Pondu (Saka-saka)

A deeply loved Congolese dish made from pounded cassava leaves, slowly cooked until silky and rich. In the DRC, it’s a comfort food and a “big occasion” dish—often served with fufu, chikwangue, or rice.

How to make Pondu (Saka-saka)

This is a practical home-style version. Families vary: some add smoked fish, some add peanuts, some use palm oil only, some mix with other greens. The core idea: cassava leaves + slow cooking.

Cassava leaves Palm oil Slow simmer DRC classic

Ingredients

  • 500–800g cassava leaves (fresh pounded OR frozen ground)
  • 1 onion + 3–4 garlic cloves (or garlic paste)
  • 2–3 tbsp palm oil (adjust to taste)
  • 1 green pepper or leek (optional, adds aroma)
  • 1 eggplant (optional but common)
  • 1–2 bouillon cubes or stock + salt
  • Chili (scotch bonnet or similar), optional
  • Protein option: smoked fish / sardines / dried shrimp / beef (optional)

Tip: if you use fresh cassava leaves, they must be properly washed/processed and cooked well. Many people use frozen ground leaves for simplicity.

Steps

  1. Prep aromatics: blend/chop onion + garlic (+ pepper/leek if using).
  2. Start the base: in a pot, add cassava leaves + aromatics + a little water/stock. Simmer 25–40 min.
  3. Add palm oil + eggplant (if using). Continue simmering 45–60 min, stirring sometimes.
  4. Season: bouillon + salt. Add chili if you want heat.
  5. Add protein (sardines/smoked fish/etc.) near the end; simmer 10–15 min.
  6. Serve hot with fufu, chikwangue, or rice.

Look for a texture that’s thick, smooth, and unified—pondu is not meant to be watery.
